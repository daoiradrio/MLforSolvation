Repository to the paper "Can machine learning predict solvation effects on energies and geometries of highly charged molecules?" (doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15002484/v1). Building on work by Riniker et al. [1] we demonstrate how simple GNN models suffice to learn Gibbs solvation energies of highly charged molecules. Furthermore, we show that in combination with AIMNet2 [2] vacuum energies and adequate feature embedding layers, this enables geometry optimizations in solution even without training on labels for solvation energy gradients.
