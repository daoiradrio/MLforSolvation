Repository to the paper "Can machine learning predict solvation effects on energies and geometries of highly charged molecules?". Building on work by Riniker et al. [1] we demonstrate how simple GNN models suffice to learn Gibbs solvation energies of highly charged molecules. In combination with AIMNet2 [2] vacuum energies, this enables geometry optimizations in solution even without training on labels for solvation energy gradients.

**References** \\
[1] Katzberger, P., & Riniker, S. (2023). Implicit solvent approach based on generalized Born and transferable graph neural networks for molecular dynamics simulations. The Journal of Chemical Physics, 158(20).
[2] Anstine, D. M., Zubatyuk, R., & Isayev, O. (2025). AIMNet2: a neural network potential to meet your neutral, charged, organic, and elemental-organic needs. Chemical Science, 16(23), 10228-10244.
